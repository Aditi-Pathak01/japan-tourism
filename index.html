Jigokudani Snow Monkey Park

Jigokudani Monkey Park is located in Yamanouchi, Nagano Prefecture, Japan. It is part of the Joshinetsu Kogen National Park, and is located in the valley of the Yokoyu-River, in the northern part of the prefecture.

Yakushima Island

Yakushima is a round-shaped island in the Kagoshima Prefecture of Japan, located 60 kilometers south of the Osumi Peninsula and 60 kilometers off the southernmost tip of Kyushu Island

Kyoto Temples

Kyoto, Japan has over 1,600 Buddhist temples and more than 400 Shinto shrines, many of which are famous and retain their traditional architecture.

Fushimi Inari Shrine (伏見稲荷大社, Fushimi Inari Taisha) is an important Shinto shrine in southern Kyoto. It is famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates, which straddle a network of trails behind its main buildings.

NighTlife

"FROM the izakayas of Golden Gai to the skyscraper rooftops, Tokyo's nightlife is a kaleidoscope of experiences."

GOLDEN GAI is a famous nightlife district located in the Shinjuku area of Tokyo, Japan. It consists of a network of narrow alleyways lined with tiny bars, each typically seating only a handful of customers. Despite its small size, Golden Gai is renowned for its vibrant and eclectic atmosphere, attracting locals and tourists alike.

IZAKAYAS are traditional Japanese pubs where people gather after work to unwind over drinks and small dishes. Izakayas offer a relaxed atmosphere and are great for socializing.

RAMEN SHOPS & LATE NIGHT EATERIES,
Many ramen shops and eateries stay open late into the night, offering delicious comfort food for those who've been out enjoying the nightlife.

NIGHT MARKETS
In some cities, you'll find night markets selling street food, snacks, and various goods. These markets often attract both locals and tourists looking for a lively atmosphere and tasty treats.

THE RITZ CARLTON, Tokyo
Situated in the upscale Roppongi district, this hotel offers stunning views of the Tokyo skyline and Mount Fuji. It boasts luxurious accommodations, Michelin-starred dining options, and an elegant spa.

FOUR SEASONS HOTEL, Kyoto
Nestled in a historic garden in Kyoto, this hotel combines traditional Japanese architecture with modern luxury. Guests can experience authentic tea ceremonies, indulge in gourmet cuisine, and unwind in the serene surroundings.

KARAOKE BARS
Karaoke is immensely popular in Japan. You'll find karaoke bars scattered throughout cities where you can rent a private room with friends and sing your heart out.
