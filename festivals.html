FESTIVALS OF JAPAN

1) Hanami matsuri
What is a cherry blossom festival? Japan's cherry blossom festivals are a centuries-old cultural tradition. Taking place throughout the entire country, these festivals are commemorated with picnics, parties, sake, and viewing parties, also referred to as hanami, which means “viewing flowers.”

2) Aomori Nebuta Matsuri
Where: Aomori-shi, Aomori:- Accompany the huge, vibrantly colored doll-shaped lanterns known as Nebuta as they are paraded through the city of Aomori at nighttime. The festival is played out to the sounds of drums, flutes, cymbals and dancers leaping to cries of “Rassera.” Rental costumes are required to participate.

3) Kesencho Kenka Tanabata Matsuri
Where? Rikuzentakata-shi, Iwate:- Large floats are pulled around the town before being crashed into each other in a stirring finale. This festival has a very long history, but serves a greater purpose in more recent times as it aims to raise the spirits of those who suffered in the massive earthquake and subsequent tsunami that struck the area on March 11, 2011.

4) Gion Matsuri
The Gion Matsuri, or Gion Festival, is a midsummer celebration in Kyoto, Japan that takes place over the entire month of July.
